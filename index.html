Snowsquirrel

Autonomous snowplow designed and built by UMN Robotics

After joining the UMN Robotics team my sophomore year, I was thrown headfirst into an already successful autonomous snowplow project: Snowsquirrel. This snowplow was annually entered in the Institute of Navigation's Snowplow Competition, where it competes with other collegiate robotics teams around the Midwest. Prior to joining the team, I was unfamiliar with the ROS framework, C++, and other key concepts to help the team. However, the UMN team was very cooperative with newcomers, and I found myself learning how to help quite quickly. I began with making a working status light for the robot to help with troubleshooting, then on a text-to-speech ROS "node" to give the Snowsquirrel a voice, and finally have reached a point where I'm able to work on algorithms to make the Snowsquirrel plow more snow faster and more efficiently, helping contribute to another successful year at ION's 10th annual snowplow competition!

  • Date Joined: September 2018
  • Club: UMN Robotics
  • Utilizes: ROS, Python, C++, Electrical Engineering, working in team environment

Gameboy Zero

Pi-powered game emulater

This project was my first deep-dive with the Raspberry Pi microcomputer. Using a Pi Zero, I learned the basics of Electrical Engineering, including soldering, current and amperage, wire gauges, and other concepts. I even programmed a Teensy Arduino to map button presses to keyboard keys, allowing for the Retropie emulator to successfully translate the button presses into controls for the game library. The project was stressful to get right, but was quite rewarding - and a lot of fun to play!

  • Finished: January 2018
  • Utilizes: Raspberry Pi, Electrical Engieering, C++

Dog Food Dispenser

Pi-Powered Automatic Dispenser

This Pi project was finished in two days, and created as a thank you present to my parents for housing me this last summer. The project aims to regulate my dogs eating schedule, as manually scooping and feeding happens at different times each day. The Pi stays plugged in, using minimal power throughout, and is programmed to turn a high-torque DC motor at the hours of 6:00 am and 5:00 pm CST. This therefore eases the mind of my mom and dad, making sure they can stress about one less thing with my dog.

  • Finished: August 2019
  • Utilizes: Raspberry Pi, Python, Electrical Engineering
